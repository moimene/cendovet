# CENDOVET — Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary audience — Pet owners (70% of traffic):**
Adults 30-65 in southern Spain (Campo de Gibraltar, Marbella, Cadiz, Ceuta) who are emotionally invested in their pet's health and willing to pay for advanced, less painful treatment. They arrive anxious — their pet needs surgery or has a complex condition — and need to feel immediate trust and reassurance. They browse on mobile during stressful moments (vet just told them about surgery, pet is sick at night).

**Secondary audience — Referring veterinary clinics (30%):**
General practice veterinarians across Spain looking for a specialist to handle complex MIS procedures (laparoscopy, endoscopy, lithotripsy). They need clinical credibility, clear referral pathways, and evidence of expertise (cases, technology, credentials). They browse on desktop during work hours.

**Jobs to be done:**
- "My pet needs surgery — is there a less painful option?"
- "I need to refer a complex case to a MIS specialist"
- "I want to understand what this procedure involves before deciding"

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** Innovadora, compasiva, valiente

**Voice:** Technically authoritative but never cold. Like a surgeon who sits down with you after the procedure to explain everything clearly, with warmth. Educational without being condescending. Confident without being arrogant.

**Emotional goal:** The primary emotion visitors should feel is **tranquilidad y confianza** — "my pet is in the best possible hands." The site should project calm competence, not flashy tech-bro energy.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Reference:** [Duke Health](https://www.dukehealth.org) — clean, institutional healthcare design that balances clinical seriousness with human warmth. Green-toned palette, generous whitespace, photography-forward, clear hierarchy.

**Anti-references (what CENDOVET should NOT look like):**
- Generic "AI-generated" startup landing pages with gradient text and glassmorphism
- Dark mode tech/SaaS dashboards
- Overly playful or childish pet websites with cartoon mascots
- Cluttered veterinary clinic sites with stock photos of puppies

**Visual tone:**
- Light mode only — clean, clinical, trustworthy
- Photography-forward: real cases, real patients, real equipment
- Whitespace as a design element — breathing room conveys confidence
- Subtle warmth through color tinting, not decoration

**Theme:** Light only. Healthcare context demands light backgrounds for readability and trust. Dark mode is inappropriate for anxious pet owners seeking medical information.

### Color Palette

**Primary:** Teal-600 (`#0d9488`) — medical trust, calm expertise, nature/health
**Secondary:** Slate-800 (`#1e293b`) — authority, text hierarchy
**Accent:** Rose-600 (`#e11d48`) — used sparingly for critical CTAs or alerts
**Surface tints:** Teal-50 (`#f0fdfa`) for highlighted sections, Slate-50 for alternating sections
**Neutral text:** Slate-600 for body, Slate-800 for headings
**Service-specific colors (badges only, solid, no gradients):**
- Endoscopia: Rose-600
- Litotricia Laser: Amber-600
- Fluoroscopia: Emerald-600
- Cardiologia: Red-600
- Laparoscopia: Teal-600
- Innovacion/Tech: Indigo-600

**Rules:**
- Never use gradient text
- Never use purple/violet (AI tell)
- Never use cyan (too generic) — use teal instead
- Always tint neutrals toward teal for cohesion
- No pure black or pure white — use slate-900/slate-50

### Typography

**Heading font:** Outfit (geometric sans, 500-800 weight) — modern, confident, distinct
**Body font:** Poppins (300-600 weight) — friendly, readable, approachable
**Scale:** Use minimum 1.25 ratio between heading levels
**Rules:**
- h1: text-4xl/5xl Outfit 700-800
- h2: text-3xl/4xl Outfit 700
- h3: text-xl/2xl Outfit 600
- Body: text-base Poppins 400
- Captions/labels: text-sm Poppins 500
- Max line length: 65-75ch for body text

### Layout Principles

- Left-align text in content sections; reserve center alignment for hero and CTAs only
- Vary grid layouts between sections — don't repeat the same 3-column grid
- Use generous vertical spacing (py-16 to py-24) between major sections
- Cards should be simple: no nested cards, no side-tab borders, no glassmorphism
- Photography should be large and prominent, not thumbnails
- Mobile-first: most anxious pet owners are on phones

### Animation Rules

- Entrance animations only on first viewport intersection (whileInView + once: true)
- Use opacity + translateY only — no scale, no bounce, no spring physics
- Duration: 0.3-0.5s max, easeOut
- Hover states: subtle shadow change only — no translateY, no scale
- Remove any animation where initial === animate (does nothing)

### Design Principles

1. **Calm authority** — Every design decision should reinforce "you're in expert hands." Clean lines, clear hierarchy, no visual noise.
2. **Show, don't decorate** — Real case photos and clinical imagery over decorative gradients or abstract illustrations.
3. **Breathe** — Generous whitespace signals confidence. Cluttered layouts signal desperation.
4. **One voice** — Consistent teal palette, Outfit + Poppins pairing, solid colors throughout. No rainbow badges or per-section themes.
5. **Mobile empathy** — Design for the anxious pet owner on their phone at 11pm. Fast loading, clear CTAs, readable text.

### Accessibility

- WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 contrast for text)
- All interactive elements must have visible focus indicators
- All images must have descriptive alt text
- All external links need aria-label indicating new window
- No information conveyed by color alone
- Respect prefers-reduced-motion
